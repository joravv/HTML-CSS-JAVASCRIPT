Fcb History

FC Barcelona, founded in 1899 by a group of Swiss, English, and Catalan footballers led by Joan Gamper, has grown into one of the most iconic and successful football clubs in the world. Known as *Barça*, the club quickly became a symbol of Catalan identity and pride, especially during periods of political repression in Spain. Over the decades, Barcelona built a reputation for its distinctive attacking style of play, later formalized through the famous “tiki-taka” philosophy showcased by legends like Xavi, Iniesta, and Messi. With historic eras under coaches such as Johan Cruyff and Pep Guardiola, Barça collected numerous La Liga titles, Champions League trophies, and global accolades. Beyond sporting achievements, the club stands out for its motto, *“Més que un club”* (“More than a club”), reflecting its deep cultural, social, and political significance to Catalonia and football fans around the world.
